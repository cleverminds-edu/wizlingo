## The Three Models at a Glance

### **MODEL 1: Per-Student Annual (₹150-250/student/year)**

```
Example Pricing:
  Small school (500 students): ₹75K - ₹125K/year
  Medium school (1000 students): ₹150K - ₹250K/year
  Large school (2000 students): ₹300K - ₹500K/year

How it scales:
  - 10 schools × avg ₹150K = ₹15L ARR (Year 1)
  - 27 schools × avg ₹175K = ₹47L ARR (Year 2)
  - 60 schools × avg ₹200K = ₹1.2Cr ARR (Year 3)
```

**✅ PROS:**
- Simple to explain ("just like Vedantu or BYJU'S")
- Scales naturally (bigger school = bigger payment)
- Schools understand value per student
- Easy to adjust for city-tier (₹150 Tier 2, ₹250 Tier 1)
- **Investors love this** (scales revenue with adoption)

**❌ CONS:**
- Large schools might negotiate down per-student rate
- Requires more tracking (which students are "active"?)
- Teachers might game the system (only use for high performers)

**IDEAL FOR:** If you want simple, scalable, investor-friendly pricing. **Recommended.**

**Unit Economics:**
| School Size | Price | CAC | LTV (3yr) | LTV:CAC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 500 | ₹75K | ₹12K | ₹225K | 18:1 ✅ |
| 1000 | ₹150K | ₹15K | ₹450K | 30:1 ✅ |
| 2000 | ₹300K | ₹18K | ₹900K | 50:1 ✅✅ |

### **MODEL 2: Tiered by School Size**

```
Small (100-500 students): ₹40K/year
Medium (500-1500 students): ₹120K/year
Large (1500+ students): ₹350K/year

Examples:
  300-student school: ₹40K
  800-student school: ₹120K
  2000-student school: ₹350K
```

**✅ PROS:**
- **Fastest selling** (clear pricing, no negotiation)
- Fair to small schools (cheaper entry)
- Discourages price haggling ("tier locked")
- **Best for school board sales** (simple to explain)

**❌ CONS:**
- Unfair to large schools (paying less per-student)
- Leaves money on table with elite schools
- **Investors less happy** (non-linear growth story)
- Hard to adjust between tiers (where does 1400-student school go?)

**IDEAL FOR:** If you want to close schools FAST and minimize sales friction.

**Unit Economics:**
| School Size | Price | Per-Student Cost | Fair? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 300 | ₹40K | ₹133/student | Fair ✅ |
| 800 | ₹120K | ₹150/student | Fair ✅ |
| 2000 | ₹350K | ₹175/student | Below market ⚠️ |

### **MODEL 3: Freemium + Premium (NOT YET)**

```
FREE tier (up to 100 students):
  - 5 reading sessions/week per student
  - Basic reporting for teacher
  - No parent emails
  
PREMIUM (unlimited):
  - Unlimited reading + speaking
  - Advanced teacher reports
  - Parent engagement features
  - Price: ₹500-1000/school/month OR ₹5K-10K/year

Examples:
  Small school (free tier): ₹0
  Medium school (premium): ₹6K-12K/year
  Large school (premium): ₹6K-12K/year
```

**✅ PROS:**
- **Lowest barrier to adoption** (try for free)
- Creates natural upsell path
- Good for awareness building

**❌ CONS:**
- Schools game free tier (use only for high performers)
- Low conversion rate (<10% free → paid historically in EdTech)
- Complex billing, more support cost
- **Investors hate this** (unpredictable revenue)
- **Not recommended for Year 1**

**IDEAL FOR:** Year 2+ after you have 20-30 paying schools and want to expand TAM.

## Decision Framework: Which Model to Choose?

### **Ask These Three Questions:**

#### **Q1: How price-sensitive are your target schools?**

**If answer is: "Very (Tier 2/3 cities, cost-conscious boards")**
→ **Use MODEL 2 (Tiered)** — Fast closes, clear pricing

**If answer is: "Medium (mix of Tier 1 and Tier 2, can afford)"**
→ **Use MODEL 1 (Per-Student)** — Recommended, balanced

**If answer is: "Low (Elite private schools, Delhi/Mumbai)"**
→ **Use MODEL 1 (Per-Student)** at premium prices (₹300-400/student)

---

#### **Q2: How important is Series A fundraising?**

**If answer is: "Very important, want to raise in 18 months"**
→ **Use MODEL 1 (Per-Student)** — Investors want linear, scalable unit economics

**If answer is: "Somewhat important, can raise at ₹1-2Cr ARR"**
→ **Use MODEL 2 (Tiered)** — Still fundable with traction

**If answer is: "Not important, bootstrapping or long runway"**
→ **Use MODEL 3 (Freemium)** — Maximizes reach before monetizing

---

#### **Q3: Do you want to maximize speed to first 10 schools or revenue per school?**

**If answer is: "Speed (close 10 schools in 6 months)"**
→ **Use MODEL 2 (Tiered)** — Closed deals in 2 weeks, not 6

**If answer is: "Revenue (each school pays as much as possible)"**
→ **Use MODEL 1 (Per-Student)** — Larger schools pay significantly more

## The Recommendation

**For WizLingo in June 2026:**

### ✅ **USE MODEL 1: Per-Student Annual**

**Price tier:**
- **Tier 1 (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore elite private):** ₹300-400/student/year
- **Tier 1.5 (Metro good private):** ₹200-250/student/year  
- **Tier 2 (Tier-2 cities, good schools):** ₹150/student/year
- **Tier 3 (Tier-3, boards, aspirational):** ₹80-100/student/year

**Why:**
1. ✅ **Simple** — Schools understand this model (Vedantu, BYJU'S use it)
2. ✅ **Scalable** — Naturally scales with school size + growth
3. ✅ **Flexible** — Can adjust per city/school type without changing model
4. ✅ **Fundable** — Series A investors prefer this (predictable unit economics)
5. ✅ **Fair** — Schools pay proportional to value (# of students)
6. ✅ **Sales-friendly** — Can negotiate per-student rate rather than hard price

**Timeline:**
- **Stage 2 (Sept-Dec 2026):** Both pilot schools at flat ₹200/student
- **Stage 3 (Jan 2027 onwards):** Introduce tier-based pricing as you scale

## Stage 2 Pilot Pricing (Sept-Dec 2026)

**For the 2 pilot schools, use this structure:**

| School | Real Price | Pilot Price | Discount |
|---|---|---|---|
| School A (800 students) | ₹150K (₹188/student) | ₹37.5K (₹47/student) | 75% |
| School B (1000 students) | ₹175K (₹175/student) | ₹43.75K (₹44/student) | 75% |

**Why 75% discount:**
- Shows serious commitment to partnership
- Gets you case study + testimonial (worth ₹50L+ in Series A)
- Covers server costs (~₹10K/month per school) and some support
- Not so low that school doesn't value it

**Contract:**
- 1-year agreement (auto-renewal at 50-75% of full price)
- If school renews at ₹100K (School B), you've broken even on discount by month 8
- Build case study + get testimonial video at month 3

**Once you have 2 successful case studies:**

### **Pricing by Segment**

```
TIER 1 (Elite private, Delhi/Mumbai/Bangalore):
  ₹400/student/year
  500-student school = ₹2L/year (double your Stage 2 pilot!)
  1000-student school = ₹4L/year
  
TIER 1.5 (Good private metros):
  ₹250/student/year
  1000-student school = ₹2.5L/year
  
TIER 2 (Tier-2 cities, good schools):
  ₹150/student/year
  1000-student school = ₹1.5L/year
  
TIER 3 (Tier-3 cities, boards, volume play):
  ₹100/student/year
  1000-student school = ₹1L/year
```

**Implementation:**
- Sales team classifies school into tier during discovery
- Quote generated automatically (no negotiation on per-student rate)
- BDEs can discount by:
  - Multi-year commitment: 10-15% off annual price
  - Referral: 15% off first year
  - Strategic/board partnership: 20-30% off (needs CEO approval)

## Discounting Policy (Hard Rules)

**BDE can authorize without CEO approval:**
- ✅ Multi-year (2-3 year) commitment: 10-15% annual discount
- ✅ Referral from existing school: 10% discount
- ✅ First-in-district (strategic): Up to 20% discount

**Needs CEO approval:**
- ⚠️ Board/government school: Up to 50% discount (volume + reputation)
- ⚠️ Large competitor threat: Up to 30% discount (to defend)
- ⚠️ Case study / PR opportunity: Negotiate case-by-case

**Never do this:**
- ❌ Drop below ₹100/student/year (breaks economics)
- ❌ Multi-year contracts at escalating discounts (locks in low pricing)
- ❌ Free trials longer than 2 months (sets wrong expectations)

## Payment Terms

| School Size | Payment Terms |
|---|---|
| <₹2L annual | Quarterly or annual (annual = 5% discount) |
| ₹2-5L annual | 50% upfront + 50% in 6 months, OR quarterly |
| >₹5L annual | Negotiate (monthly, quarterly, or annual) |

**Default: Annual upfront (best cash flow)**
- Incentive: 10% discount if paid upfront in January
- Example: ₹150K contract = ₹135K if paid Jan 1, ₹150K if quarterly

## What Stage 2 Data Will Tell You

**After Stage 2 pilot schools (Dec 2026), you'll know:**

✅ **Can schools afford this?** (If they renew at >50% full price = YES)  
✅ **Is this the right TAM?** (If 1000+ student schools, YES; if <300 schools, NO)  
✅ **Is per-student the right model?** (If negotiation was easy = YES; if lots of haggling = NO)  
✅ **Are there price-based tiers?** (If Tier 1 schools willing to pay 3x more = YES)  

**Decision rule for Stage 3:**
- If all YES: **Proceed with per-student model, introduce tier-based pricing**
- If some NO: **Shift to tiered model (harder to negotiate per-student)**
- If most NO: **Reconsider freemium model, focus on volume**
